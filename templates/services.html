Shampooing, Bath Soak, and Deep Cleaning of the Coat:

  • Begin by thoroughly wetting the coat with warm water.
  • Apply a suitable pet shampoo, massaging it into the fur to create a lather and ensure thorough cleaning.
  • Allow the shampoo to soak into the coat for a few minutes to help loosen dirt and oils.
  • Rinse the coat thoroughly with clean water, ensuring all shampoo residue is removed.
  • Optionally, apply a conditioner to moisturize and soften the fur, then rinse again.
  • Towel dry the pet to remove excess water before proceeding to blow drying.

Blow Out and 'Fluff' Dry using Special Grooming Techniques:

  • Use a blow dryer set on a low heat setting to dry the coat, starting from the head and working your way down to the tail.
  • Employ special grooming techniques such as fluff drying to add volume to the coat and achieve the desired "shiny coat" look.
  • Use a slicker brush or comb while blow drying to lift and separate the fur, promoting airflow and preventing matting.

Undercoat Brush Out and Finishing Spray:

  • After the coat is completely dry, use an undercoat rake or shedding tool to remove loose fur and reduce shedding.
  • Brush the coat thoroughly to remove tangles and distribute natural oils for a healthy shine.
  • Apply a finishing spray or coat gloss to enhance the appearance of the fur and provide additional shine and luster.

Nail Clipping and Filing of the Nails:

  • Carefully trim the pet's nails using pet-specific nail clippers, being mindful not to cut into the quick (the sensitive tissue inside the nail).
  • Use a nail file or emery board to smooth any rough edges and prevent snagging.

Ear Cleaning:

  • Gently lift the pet's ears and inspect the ear canal for any signs of dirt, wax buildup, or redness.
  • Use a vet-approved ear cleaning solution and a cotton ball or pad to wipe away any debris from the outer ear.
  • Avoid inserting anything into the ear canal to prevent injury, and discontinue cleaning if the pet shows signs of discomfort.
  • Repeat the process for the other ear, ensuring both are clean and dry.
